In September 2008, analysts were estimating growth in earnings per share at 3M of between 8% and 9% a year for the next five years. Looking at the fundamentals, it seems unlikely that 3M, given its high existing return on capital and equity, can generate much in terms of efficiency growth. It is, however, reinvesting in new assets. This reinvestment, in conjunction with high returns on capital on new investments, will generate growth.
