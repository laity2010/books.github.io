Risk Cash flows that are riskier should be assessed a lower value than more stable cash flows, but how do we measure risk and reflect it in value? In conventional discounted cash flow valuation models, the discount rate becomes the vehicle for conveying our concerns about risk. We use higher discount rates on riskier cash flows and lower discount rates on safer cash flows. This section begins by contrasting how the risk in equity can vary from the risk in a business. Then it considers the mechanics of estimating the cost of equity and capital.
